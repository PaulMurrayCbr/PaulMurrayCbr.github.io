Game 19

Day 100

Oh day! Oh day! Oh day of days! Oh day 100, in fact. A notable sort of number. Today is the day that our heroes opt to battle the Red Mountain Devil.

Red Mountain
This low mountain is the highest point on the entire island. Barren of vegetation, the jagged rise of rust-red stones juts from the earth like an immense scar, providing a vertiginous view of the surrounding blue of the seemingly endless ocean.

Red Mountain Devil, A giant Chupacabra. Oh, I also added advanced, b/c big party above level. Ought to have given him some vampiric bats to keep everyone busy.

The game plan is:

  1. Go to Red Mountain
  2. Battle the Red Mountain Devil

Part 1 goes off without a hitch. Part 2 takes a little longer, as Mr RMD is not actually at Red Mountain. While there they take note of the rocks. The vibrant red colour is not natural and not rust. They also spot remnants of cannibal activity: seashells, human bones, pentagrams. But the cannibals are all gone.

Nevertheless, the man of the hour shows up. He targets the most unarmoured character for his attack, because if he manages to drain blood he gets a considerable buff. Unluckily for him the most unarmoured character is Keigh, the monk. It does not go well.

Meanwhile, Jack P summons a small air elemental which traps him in a whirlwind. He is grounded for the round.

I think you don't get a whirlwind unless the air elemental is large or bigger, but RMD rolled a 1 right in front of everyone so meh.

Learning his lesson he targets Horse, the goat. He manages to grapple, but either Jack or Widmanstäten drops Liberating Command and that's the end of that.

I can hardly complain - as a player doing Paizo organised play, I have used that one many, many times.

Volka hits him with Call Lightning, but in the end it's down to Nix, Keigh, and Kraig stabbing and punching him until he is dead.

Well! That was super easy! So easy, they opt to explore some hexes.

Day 101
The Tide Stone
Four stone monoliths appearing as jagged stalagmites of rock protrude from the grass at the edge of this bluff. A weathered, snakelike rune is carved into the inner face of each of these stones, all facing a three-foot-high pyramid-shaped block of stone at the center of the four surrounding spires. The vegetation and soil surrounding this pyramid has been trimmed back and excavated to expose the strange block fully to view. The peak of the central pyramid has a cup-shaped indentation—channels run down the four sides into empty basins at the base of the pyramid.

Day 101 they explore more of Red Mountain. They spot a sunken ship in a cove in the interior of the island, and opt to take a look. The DM, suspiciously, pulls out a predrawn map of the terrain. There is a deep ravine leading down to the cove, and a set of steps and bridges going down. More interestingly, there is some sort of structure.

The structure, definitely, is the one mentioned in the caves of the mother - that bit where Ieana (presumably) had cleared off one of the carvings to read its text:

To Command the Very Tides to Rise Up and Eschew What Lies Below: Empower the Four Sentinel Runes with the Blood of a Thinking Creature Tempered by the Kiss of a Serpent’s Tongue. Anoint the Tide Stone with Waters Brought from the Sea in a Vessel of Purest Metal. Invoke the Lord’s Sacred Name to Wrap His Coils around the Sea Itself that He Might Lay Bare What Lies Below and Cast Down Your Enemies on the Waves above.

Furthermore, this structure has also been partially excavated by a trained archeologist. And so, being fully genre-aware of how D&D works, they decide to destroy it.

Does anyone else have players like this? Shut up. You all do, I know.

Sadly, the structure is protected by some sort of divine nuh-uh, and so they go exploring the local area.

The Red Mountain Devil’s den
A rocky cleft cuts into the bluff, presenting a hundred-foot drop onto seaweed-covered rocks and tide pools. Several ledges, each connected by rickety-looking rope bridges, lead down into the cleft to the rocky beach below. What appears to be a large nest of driftwood, grass, bits of cloth, and perhaps bones seems to be wedged against the easternmost extent of the cleft.

The path that goes down the ravine passes by several rock caves. At one point, Jack P is snared by a Cave Fisher, but an Enlarge Person and a few missiles encourage it to back the heck off.

Counts as defeating the fisher, but sadly the XP is not quite enough to get them over the magic 15000.

They find the den of the Red Mountain Devil. There is fat lewt.

They follow the path all the way down to the cove, but see that it appears to continue underwater. They also spot what is most definitely a shark. They have a blinding flash of insight: if they perform the tide stone ritual and lower the level of water in the cove, they might be able to follow the path and maybe even loot that sunken wreck!

You know, I should have let them destroy the tide stones and then figure out what to do. A DM should say "yes". Anyway.

The ritual calls for:

  1. The blood of a thinking creature
  2. The kiss of a serpent's tongue
  3. Waters brought from the sea
  4. In a vessel of purest metal
  5. The Lord's Sacred Name

Blood of a thinking creature is easy. A serpent is likewise easy - Summon Nature's Ally will do the trick. Plenty of sea around here. But a vessel of purest metal and the sacred name are tricker. The party opt to return to port Jenivere for those two.

For the vessel, Kraig heads to the Aspis traders and offers 5000gp for a nice golden punchbowl. The trader is very, very keen to accept, but the other characters quickly slap Kraig down before he shakes on it. He spots a nice silver decanter, and offers "whatever the trader wants" for it, and so the trader quickly suggests 5kgp would be a fair price for this, too. Eventually he pays 500gp, which is way, way too much. Albeit it is a rather nice bit of silverware. But whatever: they have their vessel.

The Lord's Sacred Name is trickier. They recall seeing it in the caves of the mother, but don't have a record of it. There's some DM confusion as Nix proposes an infiltration of the caves, and the DM is like "why?". Eventually, Jack P's player, who takes detailed notes every game, finds the name Ydersius in his notes. So they are set.

Day 102

They return to the tide stone. They perform the ritual. They decide that "kiss of a serpent's tongue" means mixing the blood with some venom, and the DM decides that no-one could possibly be expected to work out that that bit means getting a snake to lick the rune on the 4 tide stones. There is an almighty lightning stroke exactly like the one on day 60, and the water in the cove drains. Revealed is the wreck of the Salty Strumpet, and far across the cove under the waterline a set of big double doors with snake carvings all over them, leading into the depths of the mountain.

It is one in the afternoon, day 102 since the Jenivere was wrecked on Smuggler's Shiv.

Dun Dun Duuuun! Bring your whip and fedora, peoples. And a wand of Fireball, if you have one. Which you don't. It's time to unravel the mystery of Smuggler's Shiv.
