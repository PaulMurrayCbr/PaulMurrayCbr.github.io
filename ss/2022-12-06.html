v19. entrance cavern
The uneven floor of this ten-foot-high cave is stained with blood and scattered with pieces of wood, fallen leaves, broken weapons, and in places, bits of bone. A hole in the ceiling leads out to the surface.

They descend 20' into a small cave. Beneath the entrance hole lie two cannibals. Unknown to them, the four remaining cannibals fought over the women, and these two were the losers.

To the east, there is a passage going down.

v20. cavern of unbirth
The smell of decay is thick in the air of this wide but low- ceilinged cavern. To one side, the ceiling drops down to little more than three feet in height over a region strewn with bones and bits of old flesh, while to the other, the ceiling bulges upward in a dome shape—six circular, pod-like alcoves line the walls of the cave at this side.
Festrogs is what happens when the ghoul transformation goes wrong.

They enter the Cavern of Unbirth, which is guarded by two horribly altered ghouls - festrogs. They attack. After a fight, the party examine the birthing pods. One of them has a note written in blood on a scrap of leather armour. It contains a number of important plot points.

I am Captain Alizandru Kovack, betrayer of my crew and destroyer of the good ship Jenivere. Hell would be a welcome escape from what hideous unlife looms before me, but it is no less a punishment than I deserve. That I was enslaved mind and body to a serpentine demon who wore a Varisian’s skin does not pardon me. It is my weakness that led the Jenivere, her crew, and her passengers to their doom. That Ieana has abandoned me here is nothing more than the fate I deserve. I do not beg forgiveness, but I despair that she lives still, and that she seeks something dire on this forsaken isle—she seemed particularly interested in Red Mountain. If you read this and you be a kind soul, seek out what I have become and destroy me, and then seek out Ieana and slay her as well. And to those whose lives I have helped destroy, I can only apologize from this, my dark cradle and darker grave.

There is an exit to the southeast.

v21. the throat
A dark pool of water lies in the center of this silo-shaped cavern. A five-foot-wide ledge of stone winds down to the pool below, passing several cave entrances along the way.

The stone ledge passes five exits, including the one they have just emerged from. They go down and into the first.

v22. temple
This long chamber evokes the feel of an ancient, hideous cathedral. The walls are carved with images of serpents walking upright like humans, snakes coiling around and eating hapless women and children, and stranger scenes. Four stone pillars carved like coiling snakes support the vaulted ceiling 30 feet above. Four small cells blocked by rusted bars sit in the walls to the east and west. To the north, an immense carving of a snake’s head looms, an ash-caked door clenched in its jaws, while to the south rises a horrific mound of bones and partially decayed bodies in an eight-foot-tall heap arranged almost as if to evoke the imagery of a coiled snake made of corpses.

Well! Well, well, well! Ancient ruins! Jack P's archaeologist's senses tingle, but there's no time for that. On the pile of bones in the temple are a couple more ghouls. They attack. One of them seems particularly interested in (one of) Snid's Captain's Hats, and focuses on him. They fight. The ghoul who was interested in Snid's hat is definitely captain Kovack, hopefully at rest now.

They search the temple thoroughly. This search ought to have turned up a thing, but I missed it on the night, so that didn't happen. Sorry. Finding nothing, they turn thier attention to the door to the north, clenched in the serpent's jaws.

v23. inner sanctum
A low stone altar, its sides carved like coiling snakes and its top carved to resemble a yawning viper’s maw, sits in the center of this room. The walls of the chamber are carved with images of anthropomorphic serpents using strange, pointed megaliths of stone to work great feats of magic—transforming an army of humans into zombies, calling down flaming bolts of lightning from the stars, or parting the waters of the sea to dash human ships upon the exposed rocks of the seabed below. This final image seems to have been recently cleaned of dust, and several lines of text have been made more legible via the application of inks and perhaps blood.

Jack P determines that this final image was cleaned carefully with a brush. Someone definitely knew what they were doing. He casts Comprehend Languages and reads the text:

To Command the Very Tides to Rise Up and Eschew What Lies Below: Empower the Four Sentinel Runes with the Blood of a Thinking Creature Tempered by the Kiss of a Serpent’s Tongue. Anoint the Tide Stone with Waters Brought from the Sea in a Vessel of Purest Metal. Invoke the Lord’s Sacred Name to Wrap His Coils around the Sea Itself that He Might Lay Bare What Lies Below and Cast Down Your Enemies on the Waves above.

This next bit was just so awesome …

Nylithati, aka Mother Thrunefang.

But instead of combat, someone is calling for parley. An old, old, voice. A dead-for-a-long-time voice. It is mother thrunefang herself, finally.

"Wait! Wait!", she calls. "Please understand. We arrived here over a century ago, to colonize Sargava in the name of house Thrune. Our task was to build the lighthouse above, but even then a curse lay on this island …"

Unknown to the party, Nylithati had cast Enthrall, and each one of them had failed their will save.

As she related the story of the failed attempt to build the lighthouse and colonise the island, her remaining four ghouls unthreateningly entered the room, and took places next to each of the four party members.

Then she channeled negative energy to kick things off, and it was on.

Ghouls bite and claw, each hit carrying ghoul paralysis, and are bad news if they get the drop on you. Within moments, the already-injured party were paralysed or dying. Only Nix escaped, by turning invisible and fleeing. She stealthily observed what happened next.

Booyah! TPK! TPK! G D TPK! Uuh! Imma talkin' double Uuh! The despair around the table was real.

You see, Mother Thrunefang didn't want these people dead. Her ghouls bound their wounds, and carried and dragged them to the Cavern of Unbirth, and placed each - heavily bound - in a pod. Nylithati then carefully infected each one with ghoul fever - no save. And then she left a ghoul to guard them, to wait for their transformation.

But the party travel with 5 other survivors of the wreck of the Jenevere. Nix ascended the rope, or perhaps ninja-climed out of the cavern, and went to alert them.

At this point, I printed off the serpents skull NPCs and the players picked who they wanted to play. Ayeris the fighter was not available, as she had been a captive of the cannibals for weeks and has taken a lot of con damage.

Nix, Sasha, Jask, and Ishirou descended into the caves and made their way to the cavern of unbirth. Nix and Ishirou quickly dealt with the ghoul, for which the party will not be getting XP, and hoisted the party out.

OK, guys, it's 11, and I have started drinking. This is going to get compressed.

Everyone decides to occupy the lighthouse, which can be fortified. Snid wedges the doors. At midnight, Nylithati and her three remaining ghouls attack.

Nylithati does what anyone who owns some undead will do. She tells them to batter down the door, which they commence to do. It's going to take a while. The party, up on the parapet of the lighthouse, fire arrows. Nylithati deals with it by casting Darkness.

At this point, Nix is preparing fire arrows, which I think Jack P fires at where the door is. One of the ghouls is set alight, but Nylithati doesn't know it. Meanwhile, Snid heads around to the other barrel of pitch in the storeroom across the courtyard and begins dipping thatching in the pitch, lighting it, and throwing that into the mix.

After a bit, instead of two slams on the door each round, there's only one slam. Nylithati is like "WTF?" and dispels the Darkness, seeing the fire everywhere. So, she does what anyone who owns undead will do, and tells one of the ghouls to pick up some of the burning thatch and apply it to the wooden door, and then takes the other ghoul to shelter in one of the other buildings.

Well, the ghoul that applied the fire rolls badly and takes fatal damage. Mother thrunefang is left with one thrall. Snid climbs the roof of then outbuilding and empties half the barrel of pitch on each of the two doors, and applies fire. The roof, although palmleaf thatch, will not catch because it is sodden from the daily rain.

Mother Thrunefang exits the building the back way, up to the observation tower, and dives into the water. She's a ghoul and doesn't need to breathe. She's gone. Which is a bit of a pity because she had some really, really nice loot for a cleric. Oh well.

The rest of the night is uneventful. Tomorrow morning, it will be day 53, and Snid, Jack, and Kraig will be dealing with ghoul fever.
