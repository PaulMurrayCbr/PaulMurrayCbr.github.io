Day 77, 78

Day 79-81

They pop by Snid's grave and resume mapping. There's a snek at one point, guarding an ancient pillar, but eventually they run out of convenient hexes and decide that it's finally time to grow a pair and head out to fungus island, which they have been putting off since Game 7, back in early November.

Day 82-83
The Nightvoice
An ancient wreck lies wedged between a pair of rocks here, yet despite its age, the ruined sailing ship at first seems in relatively good condition. The ship has no color to its lines, and is completely covered in a layer of soft gray mold. Even its sails appear fully intact, although what hangs from the spars is not canvas but sheets of thick, pale fungus. Several thick ropes of fungus connect the ship’s upper deck to the island almost like crude rope bridges.

The island, although creepy AF, is not large. The fungus seems to have spawned vegepygmies. However, these simply are not a threat to a 4th-level party of six. On the west side of fungus island, they see a wreck offshore, covered with fungus - sheets and ropes of it, and a bridge of fungus connecting the wreck to the island.

Crossing the fungus ropes is not difficult, but somehow Jack P and Volka manage to absolutely stuff it up. Jack P is left dangling by a belay rope, but Volka disappears into the surf.

In all the chaos, however, one or two of them do manage to spot the nameplate of the ship.

The Nightvoice!

Cliffhangers are tight!

Day 83 cont'
Our cleric was away, so …

Volka has sunk beneath the waves, she is gone. But Jack P is tied off to a rope and being battered by the surf. How will he escape? Well, it turns out to be super easy, barely an inconvenience. Jack has a wand of Enlarge Person. With a zap, he manages the waves reasonably easily, and the party pile aboard ship.

Up at deck level, they go through the captains quarters, finding there what looks to be a logbook. Jack will need some hours to make sense of it, as it is in bad condition. Below deck, they find a … situation:

The Nightvoice is now the main lair of what its crew have become—a tribe of vegepygmies led by a hulking thug that was once the ship’s cook.

The Nightvoice’s cargo hold is a hideous scene—the skeletal remains of the ship’s crew lie enshrined here. In all there are 19 bodies here, each consisting of a mound of bones caked over with mold; each sits amid a small circle of personal objects, teeth, coins, 9 bits of jewelry, and other objects that the crewmember valued in life. The vegepygmies honor these bodies as their “birth corpses” in a crude form of ancestor worship.

But, there is little to tell. These creatures are not a threat to this party now. The uncover some loot, but the main thing is the logbook, which Gellik is after.

The Fungus God
A fifty-foot-high pillar of fungus-covered rock thrusts up from the surf here, angling away from the main body of the larger island to the west. The rock is connected to the main island by thick tangles of fibrous fungus, perhaps providing a way to cross the churning surf below.

They explore the silent island further. On the east side is an odd pinnacle of rock, which they had noticed previously from across the bay.

They cross the bridge without incident, and within the cave is a fiendish violet fungus. A skeleton is plastered against the back wall, covered with fungus. It twitches unsettlingly. Unhappily, there is not much to tell about the fight. I think it did get one of our heroes with its rot attack, but really, they dealt with it pretty handily.

Shoulda put three in there.

The skeleton falls apart into bone and dead fungus, but atop it is a captain's hat.

Day 84-91

Having dealt with fungus island and the dryad's quest, they head to Pezcock's crab, and resume mapping for the Lord Protector of Eleder. Day 85, they take a detour and pick up some Viper Vine berries for Aerys. The druid, Widmanstätten, has Trackless Step and the thorns are no barrier. He simply goes into the thicket and comes out with a double handful of berries.

On the way, Jack P finds time to examine the logbook. It seems the crew of the Nightvoice found a strange pod which infected them with russet mold. The captain decided to "carry the blasphemous pod up to a rock spire to the east of this island", explaining the whole situation.

Everything is going great, they deal with a couple of the island's Dimorphodons, but on day 89 they have a run-in with a group of six of the island's shocker lizards. This rather badly messes them up, so they return to Port Jenivere for R&R.

Day 91-92
This week, our druid was away.

Back at Port Jenevere, a welcome surprise: Volka is alive, safe and sound by the will of Gozreh who, no doubt, has borne her through the waves and back to port. Volka remembers nothing, or at least is saying nothing about it. Meanwhile, Widmanstätten opts to go back to the Dryad's tree and do druid stuff.

They give the berries to Aerys, who is happier than she lets on. Over the course of the conversation, she reveals that she is writing an epic poem, which they take a day to read. The mere reading of this poem seems to bolster their will, somehow. They likewise give Gelik the log from the Nightvoice. He is somewhat more demonstrative, and offers to train them in voice projection and proper timing, which they spend the next day doing. They buy some stuff, I think, form the consortium trading post, and then it's back to mapping.

Aaand at this point, beautiful people, I shall start summarizing.

The party map many hexes, but not much happens.

Day 97, they discover the Wavereaper, but there's nothing there.

Day 98, they head back to the dryad, and as a reward for clearing the silent island of the violet fungus, she grants them a special. She teaches Jack P Jungle Rhythms. Once per day, when summoning a monster that is arguably a jungle sort of thing, the monster can have the Advanced template.

Was considering a +1 staff for Kiegh, but you can just buy those. This reward is better than it seems: it scales.

They fight a giant constrictor snake. And they discover a rickety bridge across a gorge with a fast-flowing river at the base, but they opt not to cross it just yet. They head back to a previously-established camp.

Tomorrow, it will be 100 days.

And that was that. A lot of talky-talk, a lot of completionism. Stuff will shortly get real, but the characters don't know that.
