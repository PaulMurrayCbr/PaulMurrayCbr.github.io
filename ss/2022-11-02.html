But the day does not go according to plan.

In the morning they are attacked by two wild boars whose territory they have entered. One of them is big, but the other is monstrous. Snid manages to distract the smaller of the two by retreating to a tree when it charges him. The smaller boar slams the tree, attempting to dislodge him, but Snid's tail proves its worth. Not that it matters, because then Snid jumps out of the tree onto the boar, and attempts to calm it. This does not go well, but it keeps the thing busy. Unable to bring its tusks to bear, it settles for slamming Snid around as they grapple.

I didn't apply the grapple rules correctly, which will surprise no-one familiar with 3.5/Pathfinder I grapple rules.

The massive boar charged Jack, who was badly hurt. Kraig stepped in and challenged the beast. He and Nyx together managed to subdue it, although Kraig was nearly downed by the thing, needing heals from Jack. In the end, Nyx's precisely-placed kama slashes managed to bleed it, with a final spectacular slash finishing it off.

Kraig the Cavalier and Nyx the Ninja form a classic Tank/Glass Cannon combo. Kraig keeps the monsters focused on himself with his cavalier's challenge, and Nyx does massive damage with sneak attack and Bleeding Critical feat.

With the smaller boar left to take care of, there was quite a bit of confusion and table talk, but no - generally speaking it is very difficult to calm down a vicious (not to mention Ferocious) wild animal that is in the act of ripping you a new one. In the end, they had to do things the usual way.

After this, everyone except Nyx was hurt, and they opted to not risk anything further. They spent a day recovering, Kraig's Order of The Sheild medical training coming into play. Next day they briefly investigated the wreck, which proved to be Pezcock's old ship: the Crow's Tooth. Then they headed straight back to camp and their cleric for another day of rest on day 39.

I rolled middling on the wandering monster table, and picked something that I was pretty sure the party could manage. Just the one dire boar, not 1d4 of them. A single one is CR 4. Added the other boar because I don't approve of encounters with just a single monster all by itself unless there's plot reasons for it. In the end, I didn't have to nerf it, so I'm pleased that my DM-fu was strong enough to gauge the encounter correctly. Although I did wince when I found out how much XP I was going to be awarding.

Day 40-44

Over the next few days, our party explore most of the northwest part of the island, finding another one of the cannibal's jungle paths, which they avoid.

They cross the causeway to the first of two small islands off around the western edge of the islan'd central bay. The second island is also reachable by a causeway, but one much lower than the first - it will likely only be usable at low tide, and crossing it means being trapped on the other island for a day.

Which is a problem:

This bleak island stands out to anyone who casts her gaze to the west, for whereas the rest of Smuggler’s Shiv is a luscious green and a riot of jungle sounds, this island is a silent gray scar on the horizon. From afar, the island may look as if it is covered with trees, but up close the “trees” are revealed to be foul gray stalks of fibrous fungus, growing up into the air almost like stalagmites in a cave. Between these weird stalks, the “undergrowth” consists of thick wiry tangles of lichens and gray fungus, all growing on a foul-smelling muddy surface. With the exception of the waves crashing along the beaches, the island is eerily silent.

They all decide to wuss out.

The next few days see more exploration. The party sight the kinda magical area of the island that Pezcock mentioned - a rise rather than a hill. But it is a few miles away and there's no telling what might be there, if anything. They also stumble on a shocker lizard lair.

Hearty and deeply gratifying groans of "Oh God, not these things again!" all around the table. But what can I do? I mean - this lair is actually in the module, it's not a random encounter. My hands were tied, dudes.

They end up back at camp at Pezcock's giant crab. Tomorrow, it will be day 45 marooned on Smuggler's Shiv.
