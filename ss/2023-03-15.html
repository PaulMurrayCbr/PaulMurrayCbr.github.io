Game 20

We have had three games and a spoon shortage over the past three weeks. We have also completed part 1, so there’s that. All the mysteries of Smuggler’s Shiv are resolved. I shall just list what happened.

Ekubus the Water Mephit

The party crossed the slippery rocks exposed by the tide stone ritual and investigated the ship. The ship proved to be inhabited by a crazed but not hostile water mephit, who was convinced he was captain of the drowned wreck. Kraig proceeded to persuade him that no, actually he was the captain. Look at all his captains hats! Eventually the mephit was convinced.

Meanwhile, Widmanstaten investigated the hold, finding a spear urchin and a Rope of Climbing.

Temple Doors
Two stone doors stand open at the base of this cliff above a curling spur of rock. Seaweed covers the doors, hanging down in thick green and black sheets, just obscuring some sort of carvings on their surface.

Eventually they, with Ekubus in tow, followed more of the slippery, slippery rocks to the open double doors leading up into the mountain.

Sanctum
Over a dozen stone pillars support this cavernous chamber’s sixty-foot-high vaulted ceiling. To the north, four empty alcoves sit in the walls, their edges carved to resemble yawning, fanged mouths. To the south, a pair of bronze doors that seem to drip with blood sit under a stone bridge that passes through the upper portion of the room from east to west forty feet above. The walls are decorated with unsettling carvings of bats, human sacrifice, and the walking dead. The dismembered, skeletal bodies of three humans lie scattered on the floor of this chamber.

Serpentfolk Skeleton

Travelling up the stairs, they came to a large, high chamber with doors at the far end and 60’ above them a walkway across. On the walkway, three skeletons who commenced to loose javelins. Nix and Keigh climed the walls up to the walkway with monk skill and ninja tiger claw thingies. Meanwhile, the rest of the party took some hits and fired with missiles and spells.

Once up on the walkway, Keigh and Nix engaged the skeletons in melee. Odd-looking skeletons, with snake heads rather than human. Keigh was badly hurt and needed to jump down for healing. But, his monk slow fall made that not a problem.

Eventually, the remaining skeletons ran out of javelins and jumped down, and the fight was soon over.

In the corners of the chamber was a lever in one alcove and a trapdoor in the floor of another. The party opted not to fool with either. Up on the walkway, there was a long passage to the left and a shorter passageway opening out into a room on the right. The less climb-capable party members went for the doors at the opposite end of the chamber. This led to a passage going left and right, with step stairs going up. They all decided to head up to the right, to the room.

Scriptorium
Bits of bone and fragments of wood lie scattered through this room. The walls are carved with hundreds, perhaps thousands, of strange runes interspersed with the periodic image of a stylized rune that looks like a fanged skull.
Priest Cells
Bare alcoves line the walls of this hallway, each containing a scattering of wood and cloth fragments. A larger empty chamber lies ahead.

The room proved to be an old scriptorium, the walls inscribed with writings explicating a number of important plot points.

But shortly after entering the room, four skeletons emerged from a corridor coming off the room. These were swiftly bottlenecked and dealt with one by one. As this was going on, four more emerged from the other corridor, which no-one had blocked. However, these were regular skels and not a huge threat. The corridors led to what might once have been monk cells and a common room.

Eventually, Jack P decoded the writings on the wall. And, seeing as the room was defensible, they opted to camp.

Four key bits of information can be gleaned from the carvings.

  • This chamber was once a scriptorium where books and scrolls sacred to the worship of Zura were transcribed and illuminated.
  • This temple was built over an even more ancient temple— one that was dedicated to a deity referred to only as the “Beheaded One,” an entity that was apparently an enemy to the ancient Zura cultists.
  • Several prayers seem to indicate that the ancients made use of undead slaves created from both “humans culled from the unbelievers and slaves of the Beheaded One.”
  • As much hatred as the Zura cultists had for the “slaves of the Beheaded One,” they also seemed to despise their own kind—especially those they called the “misbegotten of Saventh-Yhi.”

Game 21

After a long rest, they investigated the long corridor. It proved to be trapped with a pit trap, so they investigated the other long corridor. It too was trapped, so they disabled the trap and continued. Rounding the corner, they found that both long corridors joined up and continued. And they failed to notice the third pit trap. I forget who fell in, but someone did, and drew the attention of one of the inhabitants of the complex – a Gibbering Mouther, a rock-dissolving, acid-spitting, amorphous horror covered in mouths that, well, gibbered.

At this point there was a rules dispute involving monsters having grab – does the grapple check triggered by the grab ability count as a grapple success for purposes of the gibbering mouthers ability damage?
Cleansing Shrine
An oval pool of what looks like softly rippling blood sits at the center of this octagonal room, filling the air with a metallic tang. Four round pillars support a ceiling decorated with crisscrossed supports and grooves, while ten two-foot-wide circular holes decorate the walls at chest level.

Beyond the pit, a door led to a room with a pool of blood. Few seconds after entering, heavy brass panels slid down in front of the doors, trapping the party in the room. And a swinging pendulum descended from the ceiling. It managed to crit Jack P, doing 15 points of Dex damage.

There were more rules disputes involving the feasibility of jumping onto a huge swinging brass pendulum blade trap. Meanwhile, peeps were unhappy about this deadly trap room with no way out. I ought to have suggested - as DM with player new to the system - some options, but the cutlery drawer had ran empty.

Someone found the button to disable the trap, and the brass panels over the doors slid back.

Game 22

This week I expected a short game, but it turned out that there was still quite a bit to explore.
Temple of Blood
Judging from this cathedral-like chamber’s decor, this area must have once been a significant temple dedicated to some vile god. To the west, a few steps lead up to a shrine presided over by a ten-foot-high statue of a beautiful, fanged woman, save that instead of arms she possesses two upraised bat-like wings and instead of feet her legs end in talons. She looms over a glistening altar of blood-red stone that seems to weep blood into a trough below; this trough of blood runs the length of the room before disappearing to the east through a set of bronze bars in front of a wide opening in the wall that drops away into darkness. Stone pillars support the roof 25 feet above and three large alcoves, one to the south and two to the north, contain complex wall carvings. The entire chamber feels unnaturally cold, and now and then strange disembodied whispers slither through the air.

Yazroth, aka Iaena, Serpentfolk Priestess

This was definitely the boss fight room, so both the party and the boss buffed and entered. Ieana the Varisian scholar was present, now revealed to be a serpentfolk priestess of Ydersius. She had summoned 7 skeletons to block the door and had cast Blur and Mirror Image. A Stone Call and a channel positive energy from the druid and the cleric dealt with the skeletons, but Stone Call leaved difficult terrain and Ieana had summoned some vipers.

Nevertheless, the melee guys managed to get up onto the altar platform, crossing the slippery, slippery blood-soaked stones, they and the archers whittled down the mirror images, and eventually brought her down.

Well.

Lots to see and do in this chapel. At one end, a statue of Zura, an altar weeping blood, a dead Ieana, and a 2ft wide hole in the ceiling. At the other, a brass grating leading on to a natural cavern. And in the room itself, plenty of ancient writings for Jack P to investigate.

The carvings, all written in Azlanti, tell the history of this particular Zura cult in three stages.

  1. The southern carving tells of the cult’s genesis in the city of Saventh-Yhi in the jungle, but is frustratingly vague when it comes to exact details on the legendary city apart from confirming that it was built by Azlanti—this section ends with the cult’s exile from Saventh-Yhi and how they made a dangerous overland journey that ended on the shores of a remote island far from their homeland.
  2. The northwestern carving takes up the story at this point, detailing the cult’s exploration of this island (identifiable as Smuggler’s Shiv), their discovery and defeat of a large group of serpentfolk who had gone into hiding the defeat of their kind many years before at Saventh-Yhi, and the creation of this temple.
  3. The northeastern carving plots the cult’s future plans, focusing on how they had hoped to earn the gift of vampirism from Zura by undertaking extensive and vile rituals, and once this gift was theirs, how they planned on making the journey back to Saventh-Yhi to awaken that city with Zura’s blessing.

The statue got tipped over onto the altar. Nothing much happened except the altar got chipped. They then noticed that this altar wept blood, so they decided to destroy it. When they did, a great wave of … something, some kind of force came out of it, and after they felt an odd sense of oppression being lifted.

In respect of the hole in the ceiling, they sent their rope of climbing up – which apparently you can do – and then the halfling cavalier. The hole led to the head priest’s private room (not entirely sure, if only the head priest can get there, who empties the bucket, but whatever), which Ieana had been using as a campsite. There was nothing interesting in her gear, but there was a panel in the wall which Ieana had not touched. But Kraig touched it, yes indeed he did, and now he has some rather nasty permanent ability drain that a Restoration won’t fix, bwahahaha!

However, there was loot.

Dark Lake
The walls of this long, high-ceilinged cavern seem almost to be ancient worked stone decorated with serpentine carvings or other eerie shapes. Above, the ceiling is a forest of slowly dripping stalactites, while below the floor is taken up by a rippling pool of dark water.

So, to the grating. Widmanstaaten made short work of this. Beyond was a large natural cavern, and water 60’ below. Jack P opted to go down the rope of climbing and swim, all alone, in the cool, deep, dark water. But, nothing killed him. He eventually found a floor – the end of the cavern rose up and out of the water. There was a 2-foot wide tunnel. There were oddly melted rocks. He opted, with his party miles away and no idea what was going on, to go down the tunnel alone. Further and further he went, eventually coming to an odd 15’ by 10’ room with a high, metal ceiling with a seam down the middle. After a moment, he realized he was at the base of a pit trap, and those melted rocks had been melted by the gibbering mouther, and that he was damn, damn lucky that the thing was already dead.

And, that was that. They emerged from the complex – swimming the now-submerged entrance. There was cutscene:

In another place where time moves differently, two women fight. One bat-winged, fanged, demonic, her lips dripping with blood; the other in full pirate regalia with a captain's hat and a rapier. They fight in a dark cave deep underground / in a bloodstained, blasphemous cathederal / on the rolling deck of a ship under full sail / in a seedy dockside bar. They contend with spell and divine will, reality buckling and bending under the strain / they fight with rapiers, a blinding, impossible display of skill / they engage in a hair-pulling catfight, complete with biting and gouging. It depends on your point of view.

The vampire hisses and claws; the pirate yells "Give over, bitch! These hearties are mine!" They have been fighting for centuries, but now the bat-winged woman hesitates and stumbles, now the vampire looks desperately from side to side, now the goddess Zura cries "No!", and the pirate with a cry of "Yarr!" boots her off the deck and into the drink.

After a moment, from the sea a third woman rises - her giant form made of blue green seawater, her face mutable, protean, not exactly human. The pirate regards her with respect, perhaps a little fear. But she is proud, and strikes a swashbuckling pose. "Ye also, Gozreh. Give over! Release me crew!"

"Gladly", the blue woman replies, “They are safe, if a little worse for wear. The souls of mortals are not my concern, but I have forbidden them to Zura. Take them, Besmara, and fair weather to you - for a little while."

All around the shores of Smugglers Shiv, the surf boils and gives up the spirits of the dead. Some few immediately ascend to the sky, some descend to the depths, but most swim in the surf, calling for help. Ghostly ships appear, heaving ghostly cargo nets into the water; ghostly jolly-boats haul men out of the drink and to safety. When every soul is accounted for, the ships depart - raising sail and fading away.

The party emerge from the temple of Zura, and climb the path to the top of the bluff. A cool sea breeze blows, giving welcome relief from the heat and humidity. Above Smuggler's Shiv, a few high, puffy clouds scud across an untroubled sky of flawless blue.

And so, the end. The island was mapped, the sailors freed, the nightvoice found, and people got loot. However, a variety of people seem to be very, very interested in the long lost city of Saventh-Yhi. And with Ieana’s notes, perhaps it might be re-found.

Oh, and Gozreh had a private word with Volka. Something about an upcoming long journey, and a small gift.
