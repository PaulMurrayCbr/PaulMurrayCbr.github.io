Day 20 - early morning

Kraig is dreaming.

You’re in a rowboat on the open ocean at night. Sitting across you, rowing the boat, is First Mate Alton. He’s obviously dead, with the wounds and stings his body displays on the wreck of the Jenivere, but still he rows. Eventually, the boat reaches an island covered with snakes. Alton waits as you exit the boat, standing ankle-deep in snakes, and then he turns and rows back out to sea, you assume off to look for more survivors to ferry to shore. But Alton never returns, and you wake up just as the snakes start to bite…

Day 23 is eventful. The wreck proves to be the Brine Demon! The ship is almost empty - looted by its survivors, perhaps, much as this party looted the Jenivere. In any case, the sole "survivor" is an unmoving skeleton with a captain's hat and a hook hand, who is clutching a small darkwood coffer. There is also a darkwood buckler, somewhere in the mix. The box is locked, but Jack P (I think) works out that the captain's hook is actually the key.

Nat 20.

The coffer contains ships logs which, among other things, detail the doings of several ship captains and port officials and completely exonerate Jask. It also contains a locket, which opens to reveal a portrait of a red-haired half-elven woman, titled "Aesmara".

Dun dun duuun!

Jask is grateful. He doesn't have much, but he offers to teach they party several secret mantras known to priests of Nethys, which will improve their ability to concentrate on spells in stressful situations. They spend day 24 practicing.

Day 25 is the day the party dig up the location on Ishirou's treasure map. It's a big effort. They dig down and after a couple of hours find a heavy wooden platform of some sort. A couple of the party begin to tunnel around it, but Kraig takes the direct route and hacks his way through, dropping himself, Snid, and I think Jack P into the pit - although Snid's tail comes in handy and he secures a handhold.

At the bottom of the pit is seawater, and in the seawater is a pair of sailors who Captain Quellig trapped with his treasure ages ago. Actually, he trapped three. But one died first, and the other two were starving, and well &hellp; that kind of thing tends to turn one into a ghoul. An aquatic ghoul, in this case.

Happily, these ghouls had just had half a ton of lumber dropped onto them from above and were in bad shape, but they were still ghouls. Jack was soon paralyzed and floating face-down in the water, surviving with a little help from Volka's deity Gozreh, embodiment of the sea.

After a bad half a minute, they ghouls were proper dead. But, not without consequence - Jack has contracted Filth Fever from the disgusting water in the pit.

And yes, there was fat lewt. Ishirou took a share, and in gratitude not just for the party's help, but for finally showing one of his treasure maps to be an actual real treasure map, he offers to teach the party a few combat tricks. Which they spend day 26 learning.

The encounter nerfs the ghouls by immediately dropping them to less than half HP. They are still bad news, but I have adjusted the xp. Even so, it appears that the party are level 3. I could reduce the hex xp, which has been 980xp each … I mean, 1/3rd of the xp has been hex xp, which isn't part of the module. Perhaps a compromise might be that the interior terrain isn't quite as bad as the northern hook. 5xp per, but 1.5 hexes per hour, and visibility is one extra hex. 12 a day bush-bashing not on a path. There are around 400 hexes total on the island, so they have knocked out a quarter of them (if we are going to be map completionists).

The night is uneventful. Dawn breaks. It is day 27 on Smuggler's Shiv.
