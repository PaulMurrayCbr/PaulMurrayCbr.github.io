No game this week. We were a player down, and two people had to leave early for reasons.

Everyone has hit level 4, so I spent the evening on herolab with each player in turn. Ninja had their character sorted, for everyone else we were paging though lists of feats from obscure sourcebooks that even I don't know what they do and which I am not using in this campaign. I also put in their current armour, weapons, and permanent gear - rings of protection, that sort of thing. I am handing out consumables as post-it notes and slips of paper.

Ranger now has a pet viper, God help me.

Kisune Ninja now has a Dex of 20, without jewelry.

Bard took Polyglot, but rather than picking languages now he will be able to learn the languages as he encounters them. Let's say its something magical to do with his Comprehend Languages spell. Somehow, the character manages to make it "stick".

Cleric didn't get much. More spells, and "1d6 for every 2 levels of cleric" effects are now 2d6.

Cavalier was at a work Christmas party.

And we have a new player. The player who played Pezcock for me has settled on an Oread druid. I should refuse - stone glide is broken and OP. But, here we are. Other players were saying they need more melee, with which I disagree. But the player rolled well, and this druid will be able to hand out and soak up a fair bit of damage.

In general, people were a little lost as to what they should pick. Pathfinder 1 is complex. But toughness, dodge, and improved initiative are however always solid choices and never go astray.

Six at the table and two animal companions is a big party, and combat will tend to bog down. OTOH, it means we can easily manage people being out occasionally. A druid casts Summon Nature's Ally, so my rule is: if you summon it, you need to know its stats, FFS.
