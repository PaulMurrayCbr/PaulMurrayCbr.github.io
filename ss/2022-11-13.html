Day 56-49
A jungle trail comes to an abrupt end at a fifty-foot-high cliff overlooking the sea below. A number of knotted ropes and vines hang over the edge, leading down to a rocky beach below.

The party faff about, working on completing the map. They discover more trails made by the cannibals, encountering a group of six of them at one point. The cannibal trail leads to a calm beach where there are piles of shucked oyster shells. Perhaps the cannibals like oysters? Perhaps pearls? Either way, it all looks too much like work, and the party decline.

An overgrown jungle path leads to a small clearing where a partially collapsed hut stands on the banks of a gurgling stream. The hut itself seems to have been made from a combination of driftwood and tree trunks, with a roof of wide leaves and strips of rotten canvas sail. The front door to the hut hangs partially ajar. Dozens of bones and skulls—quite obviously of human origin—decorate the hut and the surrounding area, each bearing numerous nicks and scratches.

They find an abandoned hut, but it is long, long uninhabited. A bit odd, actually - the skulls and bones littering the area do not seem quite so old as the hut itself.

They do, however, find something that looks like a journal.

They move camp to a more strategic location - hidden, but with access to the cannibal's jungle paths. They will have to deal with those cannibals soon. Tomorrow, it will be day 50 marooned on Smuggler's Shiv.
