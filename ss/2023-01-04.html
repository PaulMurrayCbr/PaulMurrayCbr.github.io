Part 1: Souls for Smuggler's Shiv

Game 14

Christmas break. We had I think a talky session for Game 14, movie night (and levelling up Volka), and then another session for Game 15.

Dinner was Jatz and dip and pizza.

Day 52 - 59

The party spend the entire week dealing with Ghoul Fever and repairing the lighthouse. Each day, the afflicted members take an alchemical Antiplague, some of them being the ones they looted from Nyalathi's hut. Volka each day prepares Lesser Restore to keep the worst-affected alive, as Cure Disease is currently beyond her. She and Jask also provide medical case for the afflicted, using the contents of the healer's kits from the hut to do so.

Ghoul fever does Dex and Con damage. If you die from Con damage, you become a ghoul. DC 14, but requires two consecutive saves. So, the Lesser Restore spells were to stave off that ability damage while the characters waited for lucky rolls.

The effect of long-term care is that if the carer makes a Heal check, the patient gets a +4 to their save. The effect of antiplague is that the patient rolls twice and takes the better roll. Neither accomplish much if people insist on rolling 2s and 3s. And remember: Con damage reduces your fort save.

So, it took all week before the three afflicted characters were ok. Went through a lot of antiplague and healer's kit charges. Always nice to see players running through consumables.

There were a couple of notable events.

Pezcock, in gratitude for dealing with the cannibals who ate his human friends, grants the party some training in jungle lore. The party may spend an hour making a survival check, looking for useful berries and other items. If they succeed in a survival check, they acquire ingredients for 1d4 of:

  • Antiplague; or
  • Antivenom; or
  • Insect repellent; or
  • One refill on a healer's kit.

Each of the NPCs had a quest reward, seemed a little unfair not to have Pezcock have something. Strictly speaking, I should make the characters do Craft (Alchemy) checks to use the ingredients. But (DM Handwave) here on Smuggler's Shiv Pezcock's info is very specific. Maybe if they wind up in other jungles the survival check might be more difficult, and the ingredients less familiar.

I think the quest reward is good but not game-breaking.

Oh, and there was this other thing.

The winged … thing that they have seen from time to time flying around the island flies over their camp and drops something directly at their feet, then screeches and flies off.

The party fall to discussing. What could this mean? Clearly the beast is intelligent, and clearly it is not friendly. But the Cavalier, Kraig, sees the reaction of his trusty riding-goat "Horse", and to him the matter is plain.

This is a challenge.

Day 60

After a week of work, they light up the lighthouse. And wonder of wonders, that day a ship drops anchor well off shore. She is the Red Gull, a whaler bound for Eleder - the castaway's original destination. The ship's jolly-boat ferries all aboard, even Pezcock. The law of the sea prevails: castaways are to be rescued.

And as the Red Gull makes way, the castaways gather on the stern to bid the island farewell. Kraig, though, with a determined "I'll be back". And then - then the clouds gather, and a mighty, mighty crack of lightning strikes the rocky east of the island. Somewhere within her soul, Volka hears Gozreh wake and demand: "Who Dares This?". She feels the sea stir and move, compelled by some magic, but far away, already vanishing over the horizon. Still. Such an outrage must be answered.

Eleder is only 10 miles away. By midafternoon, they are back in civilisation. The castaways make their goodbyes, and several have business in the city. After all - they had set sail here in the first place for some reason. But for whatever reason our party elects to stay together. They do what every D&D party does when they hit town.

They go shopping.

Shopping, shopping, shopping. You all know how it is.

Game 15

Day 61 - 65

We needed some in-game justification for the party to return to the island, and also everyone seems to be a little low on loot. Book says a level 4 character should have about 4k of bling, but everyone is rocking only 1.5-2k . Or that's what they told me, at least, and I believe 'em.

The next module in the campaign: Racing to Ruin, has some politics and factional shenanigans in Eleder. So I decided to make use of that. A little foreshadowing.

They party spend three days in Eleder. And over that time, they have a series of visitors who are very, very interested in a group who has been castaway on Smuggler's Shiv and gotten out alive.

Gelik drops by. With him is the local guildmaster of the Pathfinders. It seems Gelik has managed to persuade the guildmaster that the Nightvoice may very will be on the island. Gelik is … shall we say "on probation" at the moment. The guildmaster is happy to put up a bounty for the mission: a wand of Cure Moderate Wounds and a Bag of Holding. He is not, however, willing to supply mooks, and Gelik is not keen on other pathfinders being part of his expedition. The whole point of finding the Nightvoice is to get the credit for it and erase certain past difficulties within the guild pertaining to himself.

Ayeris drops by. With her is one of the Free Captains of The Shackles. A certain "Salty Jim", once "The Devil of the Sea". Smuggler's Shiv is well known to the captain, as is how sailors coming to grief on its shores are bound to haunt the surf. He and the other captains have done a bit of a whip-around, and offer the party a small chest of gold and jewels if they will return there and find away to free the ghosts or lay them to rest. But he won't lend mooks to the party either. Sailors are a superstitious lot, rightly so in a fantasy setting, and there's not one in a thousand miles would dream of setting foot on Smuggler's Shiv. Even the rowers in the Red Gull's jolly-boat refused to set foot in the surf.

Ayeris herself is still looking for viper vine berries, and asks to accompany the party back to the isle.

Ishoru drops by. He has with him a certain gentleman, a trader with the Aspis Consortium. The consortium is very, very, very interested in a usable port on an unclaimed island not 10 miles from Eleder. Particularly an island known to be unapproachable from any other direction. The Lord Protector of Eleder will probably claim the island, but if they can get the party to sell them the cannibal camp, well - there will be legal wrangling for years, of course, but in the meantime there will be profit to be made.

The consortium will act immediately to establish a trade port: 20 guards, masons and carpenters, overseers and managers, merchants. The idea is to establish fait accompli before the government can act. In return for the title, the consortium is prepared to offer a 20% discount on goods up to 2000gp worth of discount, to be redeemed within 2 years. But the merchant gets a little greedy, and phrases it as 20% discount on 2000gp - 400gp each. The party ain't going for it.

I was hoping they'd go for it and get scammed by the Aspis Consortium. Oh well.

Sasha does not drop by. Nyx is not surprised by this, considering who Sasha is connected with.

Finally, Jask drops by. With him is a well-dressed gnome and a couple of beefy, armed goons in livery. Jask thanks the party for helping to clear his name with the documents they found aboard the Brine Demon and introduces his companion. His companion proves to be the Lord Protector's cartographer, who is very interested in their map. But the map is somewhat incomplete. Negotiations ensue. The cartographer has authority to offer weapons and armour, primarily. Eventually, the party agrees to return to the island and complete the map in exchange for:

  • A suit of Vanara whipwood armour, taken from His Lordship's museum. The museum features a number of items taken from the local subhumans as part of the grand effort of extending the benefits of civilisation across the jungles of Sargava.
  • A suit of barding, suitable for a goat.
  • A nice light crossbow for Jack P.
  • A very nice magical katana.

They settle on the armour and the crossbow for the map as it is now (the cartographer has come prepared to make a copy), and the rest should the party be able to fill in the rest of the map. The goat barding will take a little while to prepare, and His Lordship is certainly not parting with 4k worth of magic weapon for anything less than the Full Monty.

I offered a +2 katana: +1 and a special to be determined, but I'll say now most emphatically not keen, although I think that's +2 anyway.

And then it's back to the Aspis Consortium. Negotiations ensue once more. The issue with the amount is cleared up, and the final deal is:

  • 20% discount on 10kgp worth of items each, offer vaid for 2 years, terms and conditions apply.
  • The Aspis Consortium settlement on Smuggler's Shiv to be named "Port Jenevere".
  • Lodging and board at Port Jenevere, for the signatories only, for not more than 10 years from today.

in exchange for title to the cannibal camp and structures therin and ""surrounds", whatever that might mean. The party sign. Yes, not everyone was present, but enough to grant a veneer of legality, which is all the consortium needs (and more than they usually operate with).

And so, back to Smuggler's Shiv.

The party return to Smuggler's Shiv aboard the Fair Trader. They resume filling in their map, mapping the south edge of the isand. At one point, one of the party attempts to order about the consortium crew, and quickly discover that the people of the Aspis Consortium are strictly business. The camp is theirs, now.

First, the party check the caves under the camp. They are empty - the ghouls ate everything edible, and they are gone now. Anything they had, they wore on them.

So, it's back to exploring and mapping. Three days in, they make camp. Tomorrow morning, It will be day 69.
