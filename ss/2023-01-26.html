The party continues to explore the upland around Aycenia's tree. The healer, Volka, stays at the tree for a bit. There's a snek and a couple of spiders. Standard stuff which they deal with without issue.

But at night, there is movement in the trees.

Volka's player was out for the session.

The wandering monster roll of 15% indicated encounters at midnight and at dawn - unlucky. I opted for one slightly more powerful encounter. Technically CR4, which is a challenging encounter for a standard 4-member level 3 party.

A tiger! It's coming for Kraig, the halfling - a common mistake. But Snid noisily raises the alarm, and the tiger goes for him.

Snid is up a tree, but that is no problem whatever for a tiger. As the rest of the party wakes and variously gets up the tree (Kraig mounted on his goat, which has a natural climb speed), the tiger swipes at Snid once, and then makes a full attack - claw, claw, bite. The claw attacks are enough to reduce Snid to unconscious and bleeding out.

The tiger bites Snid, and they tumble out of the tree, landing awkwardly. Astonishingly, the fall is bad enough to kill the tiger. But it doesn't matter, because the bite, or the fall, or both, render Snid messily and thoroughly dead.

The tiger fumbled the bite. But we are using the crit fumble deck, and the card was "normal damage, but double damage to you". applying this put Snid at -22, which is well over negative his constitution and RAW means he is dead, dead, no mistake about it dead.

At which point, we have to ask what kind of game I am running. Snid's player rather likes Snid. Not only that, I killed his character in Phandalin with a very over-the-top attack by a bugbear. There are a variety of DM magic things that could be done. We could opt to handwave the fumble, but that leaves the tiger alive and still attacking Snid. I suggested half the overkill as 11 points of con damage, although that's making up rules on the spot.

Did I mess things up? Should I have has the tiger spread its attacks around? It's just - lame as it sounds, I really don't think that's what a tiger would have done. I mean, I despise "its what my character would have done" as an excuse for playing like a dick, but … but come on! Tiger! It's a tiger. This party did a lion previously, when they were level 3.

Nobody wants Snid dead. But nobody wants a creampuff campaign, either. I mean - as far as I know.

In the end, the players found a solution that works.

The party collect the various bits of Snid in shock. But then they remember - part of the loot aboard the Jenivere was a scroll of Raise Dead, which they have not yet used. Volka has it in her bag, back at Aycenia's tree.

Tomorrow, it will be 76 days since the wreck of the Jenivere on Smuggler's Shiv.
