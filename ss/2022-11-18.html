Part 1: Souls for Smuggler's Shiv

Game 9

Diner was - I think - chicken caccitore? Slow cooked chicken, ton of tomato, olives, veg.

We were a party member down. Jack, this time. Not sure what the player was up to - probably getting laid or some nonsense like that.

Full house! Plus an extra. One of our dear friends has been giving work way, way too much of his precious time, but was available this week. I had him play Pezcock for the cannibal camp raid. Perhaps he will be able to play more regularly.

I way overpowered the encounter as a result of an oversight. The party had already killed two groups of six cannibals without much difficulty and were now level 3. I decided to make three of the cannibals level 2 rather than level 1 barbarians, and split them into three groups, each led by a level 2 miniboss.

I entered the cannibal's stats into HeroLab because I was leveling up three of them and wanted the modified stats printed out. However, I entered the statblock from the module and then applied rage. The problem being that the statblock in the book already includes rage. Consequently, the cannibals had a +8 to Str and Con instead of the proper +4. Ouch. I thought +6 to hit, 1d6+6 damage was way too much for a CR 1/2 monster.

I shall redress this by paying the DM's price. These guys have plenty of XP - ahead of the module progression - but what they don't have is loot.

Day 50

Our heroes have a big, big day.

Pezcock joins the party for the assault on the cannibals. It's on! Our stealth expert - I think Nix, or maybe it was Pezcock - scouts out the cannibal camp. They inhabit the encampment built by the colonists from the house of Thrune, who were attempting to colonise Sargavia many decades ago. There is a runined lighthouse and some outbuildings, surrounded by an impenetrable wall of vines woven through the trees - thick and decades old.

While hanging out near the path, arguing about what to do next, a group of cannibals comes along. Game on!

The fight is over reasonably quickly, and the party retreat into the jungle. But, well:

  1. They are only a mile or two down the main road from the cannibal camp. The dudes know this area well.
  2. The cannibals know they are on the island.
  3. One of the group went back to tell the village, because meh.
  4. The cannibals actively hunt down shipwreck survivors for food. They are ferocious cannibals, FFS.

So, just about all the cannibals left on the island attack. Two groups, and Klorak The Red.

I guess I need to wax philosophical about railroading for a minute.

Sometimes, it's a DM's job to kick the adventure forward. There is, after all, a story to be told.

Sometimes, it's a DM's job to poke people into playing their characters as heroes, rather than "Imma skiered of cannibals!".

And sometimes, you got six people around a table, each of them saying to the other five, "I dunno, what do you think we should do?".

It's cannibal isle. There are cannibals. Hungry cannibals. The hungry, crazed, devil-worshipping cannibals attacked. I was momentarily bummed not to be doing the big fight on the map I had set up, but meh. I pulled out a Paizo™ flip-mat™ with some appropriate terrain, and it was all good.

The two groups pincer the party. Most of the party attacks the group with Klorak the Red, leaving Pezcock and Snid to deal with the other. Owing to my issue with stats, it was pretty brutal. Much happened:

  • Volka: Party healer dealing with healer's angst - everyone is scattered, everyone is hurt, and you can't heal 'em all.
  • Jack: working to give Nix a flank. Being reduced to zero about three times.
  • Nix: got taken down by a crit early on, which was a real problem for the party as Nix is the primary damage-dealer.
  • Kraig: challenged Klorak the Red. Traditional tank action.
  • Snid and Pezcock: dealing with half the cannibals by themselves. Pezcock got knocked out, I think Snid climbed a tree to escape.

We are using crit cards, and I'm thinking that they are way too harsh to be used by mooks against the party. A party, over time, does a lot of fighting and necessarily gets hit and critted. The one that hit Nix was "you get permanently blinded" - WTF?!? I had Nix blinded by blood from a slash until she got some healing.

Our cleric got to use their big gun: Call Lightning. (I have made this a domain spell, because it is an obvious pick for a cleric of Gozreh.) The weather over Smuggler's Shiv is always hot and cloudy and unsettled - there are always thunderheads in the sky, there's odd flashes of cloud lightning throughout the day. Volka dropped three 3d10 lightning strikes, each of them killing a cannibal instantly. It mattered.

After four rounds, the bulk of the cannibals lost their puff and came out of rage (but were still at +2 owing to my mix-up). They fight hard, but are easy to hit and don't have a lot of hp. Klorak goes down. The leaders go down. The mooks get mopped up. And everyone is left alive - even Pezcock.

They are badly, badly hurt. But victorious. And Kraig has claimed Klorak's shiny sword for his own. It's magic! They retreat back to their own camp. If there are cannibals left alive, they want to be healed up before dealing with them. But the lighhouse they have spent nearly two months trying to find is just a mile or so down the track.

Tomorrow, it will be day 51 marooned on Smuggler's Shiv.
