The cook is days dead - killed by what looks like an enormous snakebite to the neck. The first mate is dead. Rapier wounds a couple of days old, but it was the euryptid that finished him off.

And the party recover the captain's log:

An examination of this log reveals that the Jenivere’s captain seemed to be suffering from some sort of madness that grew over the course of the ship’s final voyage. Earlier entries from previous voyages are precise in recording progress and events along the way, as are entries from the first two-thirds of this last trip. Yet as one reads further, the more recent the entries get, the less common they become — in some cases, several days are missing entries.

What entries do appear are strangely short, focusing more and more on one of the passengers—the Varisian scholar Ieana, with whom the captain seems to have become obsessed. Several entries are nothing more than poorly written love poems to Ieana, while others bemoan Captain Kovack’s inability to please her or catch her attention.

Near the end, the entries begin to take on a more ominous tone with the captain starting to complain that other members of the crew are eyeing “his Ieana.” In particular, he suspects his first mate is in love with her, and writes several times about how he wishes Alton would just “have an accident.” The final entry is perhaps the most disturbing, for in it the captain writes of how he’s changed course for Smuggler’s Shiv at Ieana’s request. He hopes that the two of them can make a home on the remote island, but also notes that the crew are growing increasingly agitated at the ship’s new course. The captain muses that “something may need to be done about the crew” if their suspicions get any worse.

With keys found aboard the wreck, they released Jask from his manacles, earning his friendship. Jask revealed to the party that he hoped to find the wreck of the Brine Demon, aboard which might be documents proving his innocence. It's a long shot, but he has faith in Nethys.

Game 2

I have softened some of the game rules, because this is supposed to be fun for everyone, not just me.

Story awards go to each player, they are not split. This means that we have gotten very quickly to level 2, which might be by design, as level 1 bites it big time in Pathfinder.

Setting up camp is DC 12, 8 hours, with one hour off for every 2 by which the DC 12 is exceeded. I am making this per character, not for the whole party. You still have to make the 12, but each character that rolls well can shave time off. Even characters without much survival can potentially help out if they roll well.

Only one wandering monster per day, because wandering monsters get a bit grindy and obvious.

Haven't softened the disease rules … but it might become necessary. Disease is a major, major thing - but it's meant to be.

I have pulled the critical hit and critical fumble decks out of retirement.

I am awarding the players 10xp per hex visited. The map is about 400 hexes, but the party aren't going to be going to them all. The 10xp is for each character, not divided among the party. When I ran this campaign before, the group at the time opted to get off the island as fast as possible, which was a bit of a shame as there's a lot of very cool content in the module. XP is the appropriate tool for steering players - 10 each is good but not great. And if they decide to grind the hexes for all possible XP, there's always malaria. Speaking of which:

Over the next week, the party manage to explore most of the "hook" at the north end of Smuggler's Shiv. Ayeris my have stormed off, but the remaining castaways warm to the party, mainly by keeping Snid well away from them and letting the cavalier and the Kitsune do most of the chatting.

Each of them , it seems, has a story to tell.

Gelik, like Jask, is also interested in a wreck reputed to be on Smuggler's Shiv. Gelik is somewhat on probation with the Pathfinders of Absalom. But it seems a Pathfinder ship - The Nightvoice - came to grief here, and he wuld be very keen to explore it.

Shasha is very keen to make a pet of one of the flying reptiles of the island. It seems they are poisonous, but she is confident she can manage one.

And Ishirou turns out to be something of a collector of old treasure maps. Apparently Dread Captain Quellig left booty on the island. Odd that he'd offer to share the loot, but it will almost certainly take a team of people to recover it. Quellig buried his treasure very, very well.

The party explore, garnering XP if not loot. Vox stays at camp, as she is a little unwell.

We were a player down. Oh, and the character has disease number 61. We shall look at resolving that next session.

Vox isn't the only one. Over the next week, Nyx (I think) is badly weakened by Jungle Fever, and Kraig acquires a nasty fungal infection known to cause blindness. The castaways spend one of the precious potions they found aboard the Jenivere to cure Nix, but Kraig manages to recover.

Nevertheless, they manage to explore quite a bit, having to camp in the jungle for a night, as they are some miles away from thier base. Snid makes easy work of climbing the cliffs and has crafted a rope ladder with their supplies and local wood. They deal with one of the island's bigger snakes, and locate two nests of the Dimorphodons that Sasha is so keen to make a pet of. They find a wreck, but it is The Tattooed Lady, which is neither of the ones they are interested in. There is nothing aboard.

More interestingly, they look across the bay in the interior of the island, a remnant of the volcano that this island once was, perhaps. To the west, two small islands. One of them an odd colour, not jungle green, but a purplish brown. And to the south on the far side of the bay, something orange. But they are both a good few miles away, and although the party stand on an escarpment providing a clear line of sight, they lack a telecope or anything like it.

They elect to get Sasha's pet for her.

One of the Dimorphodon nests has one adult, another has two. They opt to go for the one with one adult. A lucky sling hit from I forget who and didn't write it down nearly kills the thing mid-flight, and another hit finishes it off. Snid descends down to the nest and uses his ranger skills to persuade one of the chicks into a cage that he has crafted.

Snid is a ranger with decent animal empathy, and profession woodwork. Being a Vanara, he has a natural climb speed. Getting down to the nest and friending a chick was no problem, although he did suffer a bite.

Back at camp, Sasha is overjoyed at her poisonous little pet. She offers to teach the party some of the secret skills of the Red Mantis, which offer they happily accept.

The day of training draws to a close. The mood at camp is good. Some gather around the fire, one or two watch the luminescent surf … wait, was that a ghost in the waves? Definitely a human figure, beckoning the viewer to come join him in the surf. Gone, now.

The night is uneventful. Dawn approaches. It is Day 9 on Smuggler's Shiv.
